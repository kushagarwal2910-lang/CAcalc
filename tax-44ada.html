What is Section 44ADA?

Section 44ADA is a presumptive taxation scheme designed specifically for professionals in India like freelance developers, doctors, engineers, and consultants. Introduced to simplify tax compliance, it allows eligible professionals to declare only 50% of their gross annual receipts as taxable income. This means you don't need to maintain detailed books of accounts or get an audit if your gross receipts are within the prescribed limit. For the FY 2025-26, this scheme covers professionals with gross receipts up to ₹75 Lakhs (provided cash receipts are less than 5%).

Key Benefits for Developers & Professionals

The Section 44ADA for freelance developers India and other professionals offers significant relief:

  • Low Tax Burden: You only pay tax on half of your revenue.
  • No Audit Required: If your income is below the presumptive taxation limit 2026 (₹75L), you generally skip the audit.
  • Simplicity: No need to track every expense like internet bills or laptop depreciation. The 50% deduction covers everything.

This scheme is ideal for tax calculation for doctors and engineers who have fewer overhead expenses and want a hassle-free filing experience.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the limit for Section 44ADA for FY 2025-26?

The limit has been enhanced to ₹75 Lakhs, provided that your cash receipts do not exceed 5% of the total gross receipts. Otherwise, the limit remains ₹50 Lakhs.

Can I claim expenses like rent or laptop purchase under 44ADA?

No. The 50% deduction is deemed to cover all your business expenses. You cannot claim additional deductions for expenses under Section 30 to 38 when opting for this scheme.

Is 44ADA applicable to private limited companies?

No, Section 44ADA is only applicable to Resident Individuals and Partnership Firms (excluding LLPs). Private Limited companies cannot avail of this scheme.
