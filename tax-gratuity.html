What is Gratuity in India?

Gratuity is a monetary benefit given by an employer to an employee for services rendered to the organization. It is governed by the Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972. This lump sum amount is usually paid at the time of retirement, resignation, or termination, provided the employee has completed a continuous service of 5 years or more.

Tax Free Gratuity Limit 2026

The tax exemption on gratuity depends on whether you are a government or private employee:

  • Government Employees: The entire gratuity amount received is fully tax-exempt.
  • Private Employees (Covered by Act): The least of the following is exempt:
    • 1. Actual gratuity received
    • 2. ₹20 Lakhs (Maximum limit)
    • 3. 15 days salary × Number of completed years

Gratuity Calculation Formula

The formula for covered employees is: (15 * Last Drawn Salary * Tenure) / 26. Here, the salary includes Basic + DA, and the month is considered to have 26 working days.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Is the 5-year rule mandatory?

Yes, broadly. However, in cases of death or disablement of the employee, the 5-year continuous service condition is waived off.

Can an employer pay more than the formula?

Yes, employers can voluntarily pay higher gratuity (ex-gratia), but the tax exemption is capped at ₹20 Lakhs. Any excess is taxable.

Is gratuity taxable for contract employees?

Contract employees are also eligible for gratuity if they complete 5 years on the rolls of the company, and the tax rules remain the same.
