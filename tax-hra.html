Understanding HRA Exemption in India

House Rent Allowance (HRA) is a common component of salary structures in India. Under Section 10(13A) of the Income Tax Act, salaried individuals can claim an exemption on this allowance if they live in rented accommodation. This HRA Exemption Calculator helps you determine the exact amount of tax-free HRA based on your salary and rent paid.

HRA Exemption Rules: Metro vs Non-Metro

The exemption amount is the least of the following three conditions:

  • Actual HRA Received: The amount your employer pays you as HRA.
  • Rent Paid - 10% of Salary: The actual rent you pay minus 10% of your Basic + DA.
  • 50% or 40% of Salary: 50% of (Basic + DA) if you live in a Metro city (Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata, Chennai), or 40% for any other non-metro city.

For example, living in Bangalore or Hyderabad (Non-Metro for HRA purposes) restricts the limit to 40%, whereas Mumbai allows 50%.

How to Claim HRA While Filing ITR?

You generally submit your rent receipts and PAN of the landlord (if rent > ₹1 Lakh/year) to your employer. They adjust it in Form 16. If you missed submitting proof to your employer, you can claim the exemption directly while filing your ITR by recalculating it here.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Can I claim both HRA and Home Loan Deduction?

Yes, if you own a house (and pay a loan) but live in a different rented house for work reasons, you can claim both HRA exemption and home loan interest deduction (Section 24).

Is Bangalore considered a Metro city for HRA?

No, for income tax purposes, only Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata, and Chennai are Metro cities (50% rule). Bangalore, Hyderabad, Pune, etc., fall under the 40% rule.

Do I need my landlord's PAN?

Yes, if your annual rent exceeds ₹1,00,000, quoting the landlord's PAN is mandatory to claim HRA exemption.
