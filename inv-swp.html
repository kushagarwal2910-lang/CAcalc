What is a Systematic Withdrawal Plan (SWP)?

A Systematic Withdrawal Plan (SWP) is a facility offered by mutual funds that allows investors to withdraw a fixed amount at regular intervals (monthly, quarterly, etc.) from their lump sum investment. While SIP is for wealth creation, SWP is for wealth consumption, making it an ideal tool for regular monthly income during retirement.

SWP vs Fixed Deposit Interest

SWP is often considered more tax-efficient than FD interest for those in higher tax brackets.

  • Principal Withdrawal: In SWP, each withdrawal consists of a part of your principal and part capital gains. The principal component is not taxed.
  • Capital Gains Tax: Only the gain portion is taxed. If held for >1 year (Equity Funds), gains up to ₹1.25 Lakh are tax-free (FY 2025-26), and the rest is taxed at 12.5%.
  • FD Interest: The entire interest amount is added to your income and taxed at your slab rate (which could be 30%).

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Does SWP affect my capital?

It depends. If your withdrawal rate is lower than the fund's return rate, your capital continues to grow. If you withdraw more than the returns, your capital will deplete over time.

Can I stop SWP anytime?

Yes, you can stop, pause, or modify your SWP amount and date at any time without penalty (exit loads may apply if withdrawn very early).

Is SWP safe for pensioners?

Yes, specifically when invested in Debt Mutual Funds or Hybrid Aggressive Funds, it provides a balance of safety and growth compared to pure equity funds.
