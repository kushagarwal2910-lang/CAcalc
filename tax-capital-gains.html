Capital Gains Exemption (Section 54F)

Section 54F of the Income Tax Act provides an exemption on Long Term Capital Gains (LTCG) arising from the sale of any asset other than a residential house (e.g., gold, land, commercial property, shares). To claim this, you must reinvest the net consideration into purchasing or constructing one residential house in India.

LTCG vs STCG Tax Rates (2025-26)

  • Short Term Capital Gains (STCG): If held for less than 12/24 months (depending on asset). Taxed at slab rates (for most assets) or 20% (for certain equity assets).
  • Long Term Capital Gains (LTCG): If held longer. Post Budget 2024, the LTCG rate for most financial and non-financial assets is 12.5% without indexation benefits (previously 20% with indexation).

This calculator assumes the new 12.5% regime for estimating tax liability.

Conditions for Section 54F

  • You must not own more than one residential house on the date of transfer.
  • You must purchase the new house within 1 year before or 2 years after the sale date.
  • If constructing, you have 3 years from the sale date.
  • The entire Net Consideration must be invested to claim full exemption. If only part is invested, exemption is proportionate.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Can I buy a commercial property to save tax u/s 54F?

No, Section 54F exemption is specifically for investing in a residential house property.

What if I sell the new house within 3 years?

If you sell the new house within 3 years of purchase/construction, the capital gains exempted earlier will become taxable in the year of such sale.

Is the new rate 12.5% or 20%?

For unlisted shares, real estate, and gold, the new LTCG rate is 12.5% (without indexation) for transfers after July 23, 2024.
