Input Tax Credit (ITC) Rules 2026

Input Tax Credit (ITC) is the backbone of the GST regime, avoiding the cascading effect of taxes. It allows you to reduce the tax you have already paid on inputs (purchases) from the tax payable on output (sales). To claim ITC, a business must strictly follow the conditions laid out in Section 16 of the CGST Act.

4 Mandatory Conditions to Claim ITC

  • Possession of Tax Invoice: You must have a valid tax invoice or debit note issued by the supplier.
  • Receipt of Goods/Services: You must have actually received the goods or services.
  • Tax Paid to Government: The supplier must have paid the tax charged to the government (via their GSTR-3B).
  • Return Filed: You must have filed your return (GSTR-3B).

Order of Utilization of ITC

As per current rules, ITC must be set off in a specific order:

  • IGST Credit: First against IGST liability, then CGST, then SGST.
  • CGST Credit: First against CGST, then IGST (Cannot be used for SGST).
  • SGST Credit: First against SGST, then IGST (Cannot be used for CGST).

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Can I claim ITC on personal purchases?

No, ITC is available only for goods/services used for business purposes. Personal use items are blocked credits u/s 17(5).

What is the time limit to claim ITC?

ITC for a financial year can be claimed up to 30th November of the following year or the date of filing the Annual Return, whichever is earlier.

Is ITC available on food and beverages?

Generally, no (blocked u/s 17(5)), unless you are in the same business (e.g., catering) or it is obligatory under any law for the employer to provide it.
